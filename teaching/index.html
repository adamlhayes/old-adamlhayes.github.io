Last year I served as a teaching assistant for a two-course sequence at the Evans School of Public Policy and Governance at the University of Washington in Seattle. These courses are meant to serve as a required introduction to quantitative analysis for all M.P.A. students. I led two weekly T.A. sections as well as grading homeworks and exams, holding office hours, and generally helping students succeed in the courses.

Quantitative Analysis I (PubPol 527):

  • Introduction to Probability Theory
  • Measures of central tendency and spread
  • Sampling and sampling distributions
  • Introduction to SPSS

Quantitative Analysis II (PubPol 528):

  • Ordinary least squares linear regression
  • Introduction to generalized linear models
  • Research design and causal inferences
  • Introduction to STATA
